Word 26 / verb

Scrutinize

/ˈskruː.tə.naɪz/

不是隨便看一眼，而是帶著懷疑與細節意識，把材料逐項翻開來檢查。

今日定位

把 scrutinize 記成「inspect under a bright light」：它比 examine 更有放大鏡感。

Reading Path 核心 定義 字源 記憶 情境 搭配詞 辨析 現代 來源 回想

Core Idea

scrutinize means to examine something very carefully, often because accuracy, risk, or hidden detail matters.

概念焦點

高密度檢查，找出一般閱讀或快速檢視會漏掉的細節。

語氣質地

正式、審查感強；常見於 review、audit、evidence、proposal、code、policy。

使用提醒

不要用 scrutinize 形容輕鬆瀏覽；它暗示檢查者很仔細，甚至帶點懷疑。

Meaning

簡短定義

Scrutinize

To examine closely and carefully, especially to find problems, hidden details, or the truth.

不是 Examine

examine 是檢查；scrutinize 更像放大鏡下的嚴格審視。

Origin

字源來源

scrutinize is related to Latin scrutari, to search or examine. One old association points to sorting through small scraps or odds and ends to find what matters.

這個字可以想成在一堆碎片裡翻找：不是掃過表面，而是把角落也打開看。

Memory Hook

記憶鉤子

To scrutinize is to make the small print nervous.

當你 scrutinize 一份規格、合約或 log，你不是在挑毛病取樂，而是在保護後續決策不要被藏起來的小細節擊中。

Usage

生活與專業情境

日常版

She scrutinized the bill and found a charge that should not have been there.

數位 / 專業版

During code review, the team scrutinized the error path because failures were rare but costly.

延伸版

Regulators scrutinize safety claims when public risk is involved.

Collocations

常見搭配與語域

Scrutinize 常搭配 proposal、claim、evidence、record，語氣比 look at 強很多。

scrutinize the evidence legal / analysis

指仔細檢查證據是否真的支持結論。

scrutinize a proposal business / engineering

指對計畫、設計或需求做嚴格審視。

closely scrutinize formal

常見強調搭配，表示檢查密度非常高。

Neighbors

對立與鄰近概念

概念 核心精神 最自然的使用時機
Scrutinize 仔細、嚴格、帶懷疑地檢查 風險高、細節多、需要找出隱藏問題時
Examine 檢查或研究 一般檢查即可，不需要強調放大鏡式嚴格審視

Modern Use

現代延伸

Modern English uses scrutinize for documents, claims, spending, decisions, policies, evidence, and code.

In engineering, it is useful when the normal happy path is not enough and the review must focus on assumptions, edge cases, and failure modes.
